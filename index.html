Ys Goldt is a sound artist, composer, and author working across music, math, and words.

“We are asleep. Our life is a dream. But we wake up sometimes, just enough to know that we are dreaming.”

Ys Goldt works in sound, image, and words, shaping quiet artefacts from signal, static, and silence. Her compositions emerge from layered field recordings, decaying transmissions, morse rhythms, and fragments of forgotten speech, forming slow-moving collages that linger at the edges of recognition.

Much of her work begins in listening, deep, prolonged, and often solitary. She gathers sounds from radio drift, forest floors, machine hums, archival reels. These elements are processed through analogue tools, mathematical filters, and intuition, stitched together not to narrate, but to haunt.

Alongside sound, she crafts visual pieces and writes fiction and poetry that mirror this logic of residue and reconstruction, where pattern is a form of memory, and noise is a form of meaning. Her latest literary work is the dark romantic novel The Strange Mercy of Listening.
