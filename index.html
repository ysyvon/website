– Ludwig Wittgenstein

Ys Goldt works in sound, image, and code, shaping quiet artefacts from signal, static, and silence. Her compositions emerge from layered field recordings, decaying transmissions, morse rhythms, and fragments of forgotten speech, forming slow-moving collages that linger at the edges of recognition.

Much of her work begins in listening, deep, prolonged, and often solitary. She gathers sounds from radio drift, forest floors, machine hums, archival reels. These elements are processed through analogue tools, algorithmic filters, and intuition, stitched together not to narrate, but to haunt.

Alongside sound, she crafts visual pieces and writes fiction and poetry that mirror this logic of residue and reconstruction, where pattern is a form of memory, and noise is a form of meaning. Her latest work is the dark romantic novella The Strange Mercy of Listening.
